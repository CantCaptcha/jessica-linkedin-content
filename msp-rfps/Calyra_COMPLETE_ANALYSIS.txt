## 14. SUMMARY OF CALYRA ANALYSIS

**Strengths:**
- CMMC Level 2 certified ✅ (exceeds Level 1 requirement)
- Competitive pricing: $185/user/month ($7,400.25 for 40 users)
- Strong technology stack: ThreatLocker, Ninja RMM, Tenable Scanner, Cisco Meraki management
- Defense contractor references (Stronghold, Paschal, K2 Solutions)
- Documented 3-tier SLAs with fast Tier 1 response (10-min)
- Comprehensive MSA with clear terms and conditions

**Weaknesses:**
- **5-year contract term** (longest among all providers) ⚠️
- **Business hours not specified** - cannot verify 7AM-5:30PM requirement ⚠️
- **New company** (~1 year old) ⚠️
- Variable Tier 2 SLAs (wide ranges: 30-min to 2.5-hour response)
- **Emergency support costs** not specified
- Limited MSA depth compared to other providers (no detailed service descriptions)

**Recommendations:**
1. **Clarify business hours coverage** - Confirm 7AM-5:30PM ET requirement
2. **Clarify SLA business hours** - Are Tier 1-3 SLAs 24/7 or business hours only?
3. **Negotiate contract term** - 5 years is very long for a new MSP
4. **Specify emergency support costs** - Understand additional cost structure
5. **Validate company stability** - Understand resource depth for long-term partnership

**Risk Profile:**
- **High:** 5-year contract with ~1 year old company
- **Medium:** Missing coverage hours documentation, variable SLAs
- **Low:** Technology stack is solid, CMMC Level 2 certification is strong differentiator

**Overall Assessment:**
Calyra offers competitive pricing and CMMC Level 2 certification, but the 5-year contract term and missing coverage hours documentation are significant concerns. The company's newness (~1 year old) combined with a long-term commitment creates risk. The SLAs are aggressive but lack clarity on business hours coverage. Calyra remains a viable option, but the 5-year contract and documentation gaps require careful consideration and likely negotiation before selection.
