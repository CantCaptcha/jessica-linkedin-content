**Building High-Performance Teams Without Breaking Culture**

You hear it everywhere: "Move fast and break things." But here's what no one talks about: when you move that fast without culture, the culture breaks.

Over the last few years, a concept I've come to understand is that culture isn't a poster on the wall. It's not an employee handbook. Culture is what's felt every day — in every decision, every conversation, every way we treat each other. And that feeling either builds trust or erodes it instantly.

I've seen it happen. Companies that scale fast — they hire, they grow, they push — and somewhere along the way, they lose what made them special in the first place. The thing that attracted their best people and kept them engaged through the tough times. Gone.

Because here's the harder truth: Culture has to be intentional. It has to be alive in the environment. You should be able to see your core values in how we hire, how we reward, and yes, even how we make hard decisions. If those values aren't real — if they're just words on a wall but we allow violations every week — then you don't have culture. You have wallpaper.

When I was at P&G, we had this test everyone called the "personality test." Infamous. I hated it. But now, with distance, I understand the intent. They were trying to fit us — to see if we'd "last" in that environment. But a multiple-choice test can't capture heart, or integrity, or whether someone will show up when things are hard. It can't tell you if your culture matches what you say it is.

What I've learned is that building high-performance teams isn't about adding more processes or systems. Those are tools, not solutions. The foundation has to be your people and your values. The question isn't "how do we scale?" It's "how do we scale without losing what makes us who we are?"

I don't have all the answers, and I'm not sure anyone does. But I know this much: whatever "high-performance" means for your team, it has to start with who you already are — not with who you think you should be.

Your culture is what's felt every day. What you write down and say your culture is must match that.

What's your experience — building teams fast while keeping what matters intact?
