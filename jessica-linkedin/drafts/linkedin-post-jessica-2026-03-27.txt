**What Your Culture Is vs. What You Say It Is**

Over the last few years, a concept I knew about but rarely thought deeply about has become more and more in the forefront: **culture fit**.

When we hire, we ask two critical questions:
1. Does this person fit our company culture?
2. Does this person fit the seat we need to fill?

But here's what I've learned: a multiple-choice test CAN'T capture if someone is the right person for culture.

I remember the "personality test" from my P&G days in Cincinnati. Scary. Intimidating. Infamous. The idea that someone would test us to see if we were "nice enough," "tough enough"... whatever adjectives you want. How can they determine if we'll fit from a standardized form?

At each step of my career, I understand intent more and more: these tests measure surface-level traits, not the deeper alignment that actually drives success.

What I've learned about culture:

**Core values must be CORE.**
- They need to be written down and explained
- They need to be alive in your environment daily
- You should be able to see them as you go through your day

**Hiring:**
- Focus on core values
- If leadership team allows core values to be violated regularly, that's when culture fails

**Rewarding:**
- Same focus — core values must drive recognition

**Leadership:**
- Core values must be REAL
- Violating them regularly destroys what you say your culture is

Your culture is what is felt every day. What you write down and say your culture is must match that.

I'm passionate about this because I see more companies failing at culture than succeeding. 

What's your experience — personality tests or genuine values?
