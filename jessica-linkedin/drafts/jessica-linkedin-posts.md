**Title:** What I Expected vs. What I Found as a Startup COO

**Content:**
When I came on as COO of a small company facing growing pains, I expected to find basic ad-hoc processes that needed process diagrams and training. I thought my job was to invest time getting everyone "rowing in the same direction" from a macro level.

I was wrong.

What I actually found was that the most valuable activity was jumping into the work itself — grabbing individual tasks and making incremental improvements.

Even those small iterative changes send shockwaves through a team. People resist change, even when it's for the better. But as we adopted each one, something interesting happened: those task-level improvements started connecting organically, creating the macro change I thought I had to force from the top down.

My critical role as COO isn't about imposing perfect processes. It's about seeing the connections across individual tasks — seeing the macro view that others miss — and managing the shockwaves of change so the team can adapt.

Sometimes the best strategy isn't starting at 30,000 feet. It's in the weeds, improving one task at a time, and letting the big picture emerge.

What's been your experience — macro strategy or incremental execution?
